Knowledge Graphs as Semantic Bridges

Between CPS/IoT and Patient Digital Twins in Healthcare

PhD Lecture Module · 45 minutes

Graduate Seminar · Knowledge Graphs & Cyber-Physical Systems

The Semantic Bridge Thesis

  • Heterogeneous CPS/IoT streams are syntactically rich but semantically poor; KGs supply the missing meaning layer.
  • A KG makes observations queryable, reason-able, and clinically comparable — the substrate on which a Twin is simulated.
  • Lecture thesis: the quality of the bridge (ontologies + pipelines + reasoning), not the sensors, determines clinical trustworthiness.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

1 · Foundations & Standards

10 minutes — “What language do the sensors speak?”

  • Goal: establish the ontology vocabulary and the conceptual architecture before any pipeline.
  • Anchored by Hendawi & Li (2024) — clinical terminologies; Chatterjee et al. (2024) — sensor ontologies; Carbonaro et al. (2023) — blueprint.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

Three standards bodies (HL7, W3C, IHTSDO/SNOMED) ship at different speeds. Whose release cadence becomes the bottleneck for a 2026 clinical deployment?

Reference. Hendawi, R., & Li, J. (2024). Comprehensive personal health knowledge graph for effective management and utilization of personal health data. In Proceedings of IEEE AIMHC (pp. 1–8). IEEE. https://doi.org/10.1109/aimhc59811.2024.00026

Healthcare Ontology Foundations: From Codes to Graphs

  • Core contribution: A Comprehensive Personal Health KG (PHKG) unifying EHR, wearables, insurance, and social determinants of health (SDoH) under one OWL schema with SNOMED CT + ICD-10 mapped to nodes.
  • Privacy is schema-native (role-based access control embedded in the KG), not bolted on; SPARQL exposes longitudinal/temporal queries.
  • Ontology used: OWL · SNOMED CT (clinical) · ICD-10 (administrative).

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

SNOMED CT (post-coordinated clinical detail) and ICD-10 (billing granularity) describe the same patient at different resolutions. Where does information irreversibly leak in this mapping — and can SDoH (no dominant vocabulary) ever be first-class?

Reference. Hendawi, R., & Li, J. (2024). Comprehensive personal health knowledge graph for effective management and utilization of personal health data. In Proceedings of IEEE AIMHC (pp. 1–8). IEEE. https://doi.org/10.1109/aimhc59811.2024.00026

Sensor Semantics: The SSN/SOSA Bridge

  • Core contribution: Annotates MOX2-5 accelerometer observations as OWL/RDF triples via W3C SSN/SOSA; converts CSV → RDF and compares real vs. synthetic activity data through SPARQL.
  • Formally models sensor · observation · feature-of-interest · result, yielding device-agnostic, interoperable activity graphs with an explicit FHIR integration pathway.
  • Ontology used: W3C SSN/SOSA (observation structure), candidate FHIR alignment.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

SSN/SOSA describes how an observation was made but not what it means clinically. Where, exactly, does the SOSA-to-FHIR/Observation mapping lose the physiological context the Twin needs?

Reference. Chatterjee, A., Gerdes, M., Prinz, A., Riegler, M., & Martínez, S. (2024). Semantic representation and comparative analysis of physical activity sensor observations using MOX2-5 sensor in real and synthetic datasets: A proof-of-concept study. Scientific Reports, 14, Article 55183. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-55183-6

Conceptual Blueprint: CONNECTED (PKG + DT)

  • Core contribution (seminal 2023): A systematic review distilled into a layered architecture — CPS/IoT acquisition → OWL/FHIR semantic alignment → Personal Knowledge Graph (evolving) → DT simulation.
  • Formalises four requirements rarely co-located: semantic interoperability, privacy-by-design, real-time data fusion, temporal reasoning.
  • Ontology used: OWL · FHIR (alignment layer); DT engine sits above the PKG.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

CONNECTED is conceptual with no reference implementation. Which of its four requirements is most under-specified today — and would a federated DT-to-PKG link even be auditable under GDPR Art. 22 (automated decisions)?

Reference. Carbonaro, A., Marfoglia, A., Nardini, F., & Mellone, S. (2023). CONNECTED: Leveraging digital twins and personal knowledge graphs in healthcare digitalization. Frontiers in Digital Health, 5, Article 1322428. https://doi.org/10.3389/fdgth.2023.1322428

Ontology Stacking: One Patient, Many Vocabularies

  • FHIR = exchange spine · SNOMED CT = clinical semantics · ICD-10 = administrative granularity · SSN/SOSA = observation provenance · OWL = the logic that binds them.
  • No single standard is canonical; the KG is where they are reconciled — which makes alignment the failure-prone bottleneck.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

If you could declare exactly one vocabulary the “spine” of a national PDT platform, which do you choose — and what do you sacrifice from the others?

References. Hendawi & Li (2024), https://doi.org/10.1109/aimhc59811.2024.00026 · Chatterjee et al. (2024), https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-55183-6 · Carbonaro et al. (2023), https://doi.org/10.3389/fdgth.2023.1322428

2 · CPS-to-KG Integration Pipelines

15 minutes — “From a voltage on a wrist to a triple in a graph”

  • The bridge is itself an engineered artifact: ingestion → mapping → triple materialisation → query.
  • Four architectures, one spectrum: how much happens at the edge vs. centrally, and how often.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

Before seeing the pipelines: design your own real-time vs. batch cut-over point. What is the maximum latency a hypoglycaemia-alerting pipeline can tolerate before it is clinically useless?

Reference. Pourvahab, M., Monteiro, A., Pais, S., & Pombo, N. (2025). A unified semantic framework for IoT-healthcare data interoperability: A graph-based machine learning approach using RDF and R2RML. In Proceedings of the ACM Symposium on Applied Computing (Article 3757054). ACM. https://doi.org/10.1145/3756681.3757054

The Generic Sensor-to-Triple Pipeline

  • Every Section-2 paper is an instantiation of stages B→D; they differ in mapping language, where materialisation occurs, and update frequency.
  • The honest engineering question is not “RDF or not?” but “real-time materialisation vs. batch re-build?”

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

At which stage would you insert a provenance/quality stamp (e.g., sensor calibration, trust score)? Argue why doing it earlier or later changes the downstream reasoning.

References. Pourvahab et al. (2025), https://doi.org/10.1145/3756681.3757054 · Chatterjee et al. (2024), https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-55183-6

Unified Semantic Framework: RDF + R2RML Ingestion

  • Architecture (Pourvahab et al., 2025): R2RML declaratively maps relational healthcare data into OWL-aligned triples; a distributed graph-construction layer absorbs real-time IoT volume.
  • Transformation steps: relational row → R2RML template → RDF triple → OWL-classified node → FHIR-compatible vocabulary.
  • Real-time vs. batch trade-off: ingestion is real-time, but the graph-ML inference is batch over the constructed KG → low-latency alerts need a streaming bypass.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

R2RML assumes a stable relational schema. Healthcare schemas drift (new LOINC/FHIR versions). How do you version an R2RML mapping without re-tripling historical patients?

Reference. Pourvahab, M., Monteiro, A., Pais, S., & Pombo, N. (2025). A unified semantic framework for IoT-healthcare data interoperability: A graph-based machine learning approach using RDF and R2RML. In Proceedings of the ACM Symposium on Applied Computing (Article 3757054). ACM. https://doi.org/10.1145/3756681.3757054

Wearable CPS-to-KG Construction (SSN/SOSA)

  • Architecture (Chatterjee et al., 2024): a concrete CSV→RDF pipeline — the most explicit “how to triplify a wearable” reference in the set.
  • Transformation steps: raw accelerometry → activity classification → SOSA sosa:Observation with hasResult → linked to FeatureOfInterest (patient).
  • Real-time vs. batch trade-off: proof-of-concept is offline/batch (file export); no native streaming → gains simplicity & reproducibility, loses latency, so unsuitable as-is for acute monitoring.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

This pipeline reads CSV exports. What is the minimal change to make it stream-native — and does adding an MQTT/Kafka hop reintroduce a semantic gap the SOSA layer was meant to close?

Reference. Chatterjee, A., Gerdes, M., Prinz, A., Riegler, M., & Martínez, S. (2024). Semantic representation and comparative analysis of physical activity sensor observations using MOX2-5 sensor in real and synthetic datasets: A proof-of-concept study. Scientific Reports, 14, Article 55183. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-55183-6

IoT-KG-Digital-Twin: A Three-Layer Pipeline

  • Architecture (Gyrard et al., 2024): sensor layer → semantic-annotation layer (IoT-Lite + SOSA/SSN) → KG-driven DT that models patient mental-health trajectories.
  • Transformation steps: physiological signal → SOSA observation → KG node typed by SNOMED CT → temporal SPARQL detects burnout/depression patterns.
  • Real-time vs. batch trade-off: continuous emotion monitoring is near-real-time, but reasoning is query-driven (semi-batch) → good for screening, insufficient for crisis intervention.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

Emotion/affect lacks a clean SNOMED CT representation. If the clinical vocabulary can’t name the thing you’re sensing, is the DT reasoning over a well-founded model at all?

Reference. Gyrard, A., Mohammadi, S., Gaur, M., & Kung, A. (2024). IoT-based preventive mental health using knowledge graphs and standards for better well-being [Preprint]. arXiv. https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2406.13791

FHIR-Native Ingestion & Model Binding

  • Architecture (Marfoglia et al., 2026): a FHIR R4 REST API retrieves time-stamped EHR/IoT streams into a persistent OWL-DL KG; the novel MIMO ontology standardises every AI model’s inputs/outputs for transparent, reusable composition.
  • Transformation steps: FHIR resource → OWL-DL assertion → MIMO interface match → manifest-driven binding → multi-AI pipeline.
  • Real-time vs. batch trade-off: real-time API retrieval + automatic gap detection when sensor coverage is insufficient, but OWL-DL inference latency under load is the throttle.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

MIMO makes models pluggable. Does plug-ability erode clinical accountability — if a stroke-risk model is hot-swapped at runtime, who signs off on the prediction that triggered an intervention?

Reference. Marfoglia, A., D’Errico, C., Mellone, S., & Carbonaro, A. (2026). A knowledge graph-driven framework for deploying AI-powered patient digital twins. Future Generation Computer Systems, Article 108380. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.future.2026.108380

Real-Time vs. Batch: Cross-Architecture Trade-offs

Architecture Ingestion Materialisation Reasoning cadence Best fit
Pourvahab 2025 (RDF/R2RML) real-time (distributed) on-write batch ML large-scale analytics
Chatterjee 2024 (CSV→SOSA) offline/batch on-load query-time research / reproducibility
Gyrard 2024 (3-layer) near-real-time on-annotation semi-batch SPARQL screening / prevention
Marfoglia 2026 (FHIR+MIMO) real-time API on-retrieval on-demand OWL-DL clinical decision support
  • Universal pattern: ingestion moves toward real-time; reasoning stays batch/periodic — the gap between them is the clinical-latency bottleneck.
  • None of the four provides streaming incremental reasoning with bounded guarantees.

3 · Digital Twin & AI Reasoning

12 minutes — “A graph that thinks — but how, and is it right?”

  • Reasoning families in play: rule-based (OWL-DL / SWRL), neuro-symbolic / RAG, and query-driven temporal inference.
  • For each: mechanism → validation methodology → clinical applicability gap.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

“Reasoning” is doing a lot of work here. Before the slides: which mechanism would you trust to change a drug dose unaided — and why is your intuition probably wrong?

Reference. Krämer, B. J. (2025). A semantic framework for patient digital twins in chronic care [Preprint]. arXiv. https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2510.09134

The Reasoning Mechanism Landscape

  • Rule-based (OWL-DL/SWRL): transparent, auditable, but brittle & authoring-heavy — used by Marfoglia (2026), Krämer (2025), Sarani Rad (2024).
  • Neuro-symbolic / RAG: emerging for clinical QA; trades some auditability for adaptivity — not yet grounded in this corpus’s deployed systems.
  • Common gap across all: scarce prospective clinical validation.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

A rules engine is explainable but slow to evolve; a neuro-symbolic model adapts but hides logic. For a regulated PDT, which side of the trade-off does the law force you onto?

References. Marfoglia et al. (2026), https://doi.org/10.1016/j.future.2026.108380 · Krämer (2025), https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2510.09134 · Sarani Rad et al. (2024), https://doi.org/10.3390/jpm14040359

OWL-DL + MIMO Reasoning for Patient Digital Twins

  • Reasoning mechanism (Marfoglia et al., 2026): OWL-DL inference enforces semantic consistency and auto-triggers gap detection when sensor coverage is insufficient; the MIMO ontology standardises AI model I/O for dynamic, manifest-driven multi-model composition.
  • Validation: benchmarked end-to-end on stroke-risk prediction with real-world, time-aware clinical streams → demonstrates KG-to-PDT scalability.
  • Clinical applicability gap: no prospective trial; generalisation beyond stroke untested; OWL-DL reasoning latency under concurrent load unquantified.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

MIMO guarantees interface compatibility, not semantic correctness. Two models with matching I/O can disagree ontologically — how would you detect that before a twin silently contradicts itself?

Reference. Marfoglia, A., D’Errico, C., Mellone, S., & Carbonaro, A. (2026). A knowledge graph-driven framework for deploying AI-powered patient digital twins. Future Generation Computer Systems, Article 108380. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.future.2026.108380

Federated SWRL Reasoning: Privacy-First Patient Twins

  • Reasoning mechanism (Krämer, 2025): the Patient Medical Digital Twin (PMDT) in OWL 2.0 with five clinical Blueprints; SWRL rules executed at the edge over multimodal IoT time-series; only OWL class assertions (never raw data) are propagated centrally.
  • Validation: synthetic patient scenarios in diabetes & heart failure — checks completeness of ontological coverage and soundness of temporal inference chains.
  • Clinical applicability gap: synthetic-only, no real deployment; SWRL coverage depends on expert rule authoring; federated reasoning untested at scale.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

Propagating only class assertions looks privacy-safe — but OWL inferences can re-identify a rare patient. When does “no raw data” still leak a patient?

Reference. Krämer, B. J. (2025). A semantic framework for patient digital twins in chronic care [Preprint]. arXiv. https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2510.09134

PHKG-Driven Diabetes Twin: Reasoning on a Live Graph

  • Reasoning mechanism (Sarani Rad et al., 2024): a Personal Health KG (PHKG) is the digital twin’s substrate — it auto-updates as CGM/EHR/PROM observations arrive; OWL reasoning infers hypoglycaemia-risk patterns and medication-adjustment cues (HL7 FHIR for exchange).
  • Validation: evaluated on a real patient cohort; superior glycaemic-outcome prediction vs. rule-based baselines.
  • Clinical applicability gap: single disease, retrospective cohort, no prospective RCT; PHKG update latency under high-frequency CGM unaddressed.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

The PHKG updates incrementally — but a stale edge can flip a hypoglycaemia inference. Design a temporal-validity mechanism: when does a triple “expire”?

Reference. Sarani Rad, F., Hendawi, R., Yang, X., & Li, J. (2024). Personalized diabetes management with digital twins: A patient-centric knowledge graph approach. Journal of Personalized Medicine, 14(4), Article 359. https://doi.org/10.3390/jpm14040359

Reasoning Cross-Check: Mental-Health Twin

  • Reasoning mechanism (Gyrard et al., 2024): temporal SPARQL queries against the patient KG detect burnout/depression trajectories; SOSA/SSN + SNOMED CT + FHIR enable cross-domain clinical joins.
  • Validation: proof-of-concept use-case demonstration linked to SDG-3 targets — no clinical-cohort benchmark.
  • Clinical applicability gap: affect lacks clean SNOMED CT representation → reasoning rests on under-specified clinical semantics; intervention triggering unvalidated.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

If the KG can’t crisply represent “burnout trajectory,” is the Twin simulating a patient or merely a sensor-derived proxy? How would you measure the semantic gap?

Reference. Gyrard, A., Mohammadi, S., Gaur, M., & Kung, A. (2024). IoT-based preventive mental health using knowledge graphs and standards for better well-being [Preprint]. arXiv. https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2406.13791

Scalability × Temporal Reasoning

  • The contradiction: CPS streams demand fresh graphs; OWL-DL/SWRL reasoning is non-incremental → re-materialisation cost grows with graph size.
  • Only Pourvahab (2025) and Marfoglia (2026) tackle scale, and neither provides streaming incremental reasoning with bounded latency.
  • Temporal support is mostly query-time (SPARQL) or scenario-based (Krämer), not a first-class temporal model (e.g., valid-time RDF).

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

Design a temporal KG where a triple carries [t_start, t_end) validity and reasoners update only the delta. What breaks first — the OWL-DL classifier, the SPARQL index, or the clinical timestamp semantics?

References. Pourvahab et al. (2025), https://doi.org/10.1145/3756681.3757054 · Marfoglia et al. (2026), https://doi.org/10.1016/j.future.2026.108380 · Krämer (2025), https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2510.09134

Privacy-Preserving & Federated KGs

  • Best in class: Krämer (2025) propagates only OWL class assertions; Hendawi & Li (2024) embed RBAC in the schema; Carbonaro (2023) elevates privacy-by-design to a requirement.
  • Collective gap: no differential privacy, secure multi-party computation, or verifiable federation over health KGs; inference-based re-identification of rare patients is unanalysed.
  • Consent + governance (CONNECTED’s open agenda) remain unimplemented in any reviewed system.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

Federated learning hides gradients, not graph structure. What does a patient’s KG topology alone reveal about their diagnosis — even with zero raw values?

References. Krämer (2025), https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2510.09134 · Hendawi & Li (2024), https://doi.org/10.1109/aimhc59811.2024.00026 · Carbonaro et al. (2023), https://doi.org/10.3389/fdgth.2023.1322428

Evaluation Benchmarks: The Missing Yardstick

  • Each paper invents its own dataset/metric — interop metrics (Pourvahab), SPARQL coverage (Chatterjee), cohort AUC (Sarani Rad), scalability bench (Marfoglia), synthetic completeness (Krämer).
  • There is no shared CPS-KG-PDT benchmark measuring semantic interoperability and inference latency and twin fidelity together.
  • Synthetic-only validation (Krämer, Gyrard) cannot certify clinical soundness.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

Define the three metrics a community benchmark must mandate so that two different KG-PDT systems become genuinely comparable. What does each metric deliberately exclude?

References. Pourvahab et al. (2025) · Chatterjee et al. (2024) · Sarani Rad et al. (2024) · Marfoglia et al. (2026) · Krämer (2025) · Gyrard et al. (2024) — full DOIs on the References slide.

Three Open PhD Research Questions

RQ1 — Streaming Temporal Reasoning

How can we perform incremental temporal reasoning (valid-time RDF + delta-aware OWL/SWRL) over CPS/IoT streams with bounded, clinically-tolerable latency — instead of periodic batch re-materialisation?

RQ2 — Verifiably-Private Federated KG Reasoning

Can we design federated neuro-symbolic reasoning that never exposes raw patient triples or re-identifying graph structure, with provable privacy guarantees and full auditability (GDPR Art. 22)?

RQ3 — A Shared CPS-KG-PDT Benchmark

What benchmark suite (datasets, interop/latency/fidelity metrics, baselines) would let heterogeneous KG-PDT architectures be compared on equal terms — and what synthetic→real data ladder makes it clinically credible?

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

Pick one of RQ1–RQ3. State its single biggest feasibility risk, and name the paper in this corpus whose gap it most directly inherits.

Key Takeaways

  • KGs are the bridge — but the bridge’s quality is set by ontology alignment, not sensor count.
  • Ingestion is real-time; reasoning is not — that gap is the clinical-latency problem.
  • Rule-based reasoning dominates; neuro-symbolic/RAG remain promising but ungrounded clinically.
  • Privacy and benchmarks are the twin blind spots of the entire corpus.
  • A defensible PhD lives where the matrix has a ✗ on a deployment-critical axis.

Discussion Prompt for PhD Students

State, in one sentence, the research question your thesis would attack — then check: does it sit on a ✗ cell from the synthesis matrix? If not, is it actually PhD-sized?
